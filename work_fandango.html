FANDANGO EVENTS

Fandango is introducing a new Events feature which will include local events and a digital pass for venue access.

This was a group project at General Assembly delivered in 2 weeks.

PROJECT BRIEF

Our client, Fandango, is known for selling movie tickets online. They have decided to expand the market to include notifying and purchasing concerts/local events with the capability to enter venues with digital pass.

Our team, TKTY (tickety), was hired to build a responsive website for this new feature which will be introduced in the same style of the existing brand identity.

CLIENT BRAND STUDY

We looked into the existing Fandango brand as well as their competitors. We wrote down our findings and sorted them into categories to organize our thinking.

FINDINGS

  • The existing Fandango website is not responsive; web and mobile designs are inconsistent
  • Calendar view for planners and go now for spontaneous users
  • List view/map view option
  • Guest checkout alway available
  • Time limit on checkout process

USER BEHAVIOR STUDY

To better understand people's general behavior on going to events, we created a survey (with lots of debates on the tone of the questions), and we got 99 responses with the help of social media.

MAJORITY OF THE USERS

  • Find local events through word of mouth
  • Buy tickets from desktop/laptop
  • Use digital tickets
  • Planner type

We followed up and interviewed 12 people from the survey response to get more in-depth understanding. From there, we analyzed our users' behaviors and came up with three types of users to focus on.

DEFINE PRODUCT

We see lots of potential in mobile usage and the mix of using computer when it comes to buying tickets, so we decided to focus on building a mobile first website for now.

After studying both the client's business needs and users' needs, we called out the most basic requirements we are building for this product.

THE MUST HAVES

  • Event/venue detail information
  • Easy way to find/search local events
  • Inform users of upcoming events
  • Purchase tickets via multiple devices
  • Inlcude various ways to receive tickets: digital, print, will call
  • Always include guest checkout
  • Ability to log in for more benefits

PROTOTYPES

We quickly mocked up our product in paper to see if it meets our requirements. Then we moved on to the wireframes to give it more structure.

At this stage, we kept our persona user, David, in mind, and we thought about the steps he might take when using our website. We included a calendar feature for him which also would benefit Penny and Martha.



To avoid surprising the existing Fandango customers who come to the site for movies, we added the Events section that follows the same layout as the current website and added more visual elements.

TESTING

With some of the testing from wireframe prototype, we went on polishing it with images and more visual elements. People's overall reaction was: confused by icons and not sure where to click.

    SOME MAJOR ISSUES

  • Users stuck on the front page because they are not sure where to go for more events
  • Not sure what the hamburger menu means
  • Nobody notices the map view (but excited to see it when we show them)
  • Thought zip code search is the map view because of the icon

    MAJOR FIX

  • Clearly labeled icons
  • Moved the filter and map view buttons to make it less cluster with calendar

After getting feedback from user testing, we tried to redesign the _mainpage which confused many people. We came up with two designs and asked people which one they like. The result was 50 and 50, which... didn't help.

So, we decided to go along with the image carousel style that the existing Fandango desktop website has to keep the designs consistent. We also cleaned up the graphics a bit and made the navigation easier.
